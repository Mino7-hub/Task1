Elden Ring – The Must Play Game

Storyline & Narrative: 9/10

Elden Ring offers a rich and immersive storyline set in the **Lands Between**, a vast open world filled with mystery, danger, and dark secrets. Unlike many traditional RPGs, **Elden Ring’s** narrative is told indirectly, through environmental storytelling, cryptic NPC dialogue, and item descriptions. The game encourages players to piece together its lore at their own pace, rewarding curiosity and exploration.

The story revolves around the shattered **Elden Ring** and the role of the **Tarnished**, a character seeking to restore or reshape the Ring’s power. The narrative is deeply rooted in **fantasy tropes** and **mythology**, but it’s uniquely told in a way that feels more like discovering a legend than being handed a direct storyline. Some may find the lack of traditional narrative structure jarring, but for those who enjoy uncovering the world’s secrets, this method is both engaging and mysterious.

Gameplay & Mechanics: 10/10

Elden Ring’s gameplay is a seamless blend of exploration, combat, and RPG elements. The game is an evolution of the mechanics introduced in FromSoftware’s previous titles, like **Dark Souls** and **Bloodborne**, but with the addition of an expansive, open-world design. The combat is fluid, challenging, and deeply satisfying, with the player having a wide range of weapons, magic, and abilities to choose from.

  • Combat: The combat in **Elden Ring** is challenging yet rewarding, requiring patience, skill, and strategic thinking. The controls are responsive, and while combat can feel unforgiving at times, mastering the mechanics is immensely satisfying. There’s a high degree of variety in the combat, with melee, ranged, and magic playstyles all being viable, and the game encourages experimentation with different builds.
  • Exploration: The open world of the **Lands Between** is vast, beautifully designed, and filled with secrets. Players can travel freely across the world, encountering various regions, from desolate swamps to towering castles, all while battling formidable enemies and discovering new areas. The world feels alive and interconnected, with hidden paths, caves, and dungeons to explore. The map is large, yet it doesn’t overwhelm players, and there’s always something intriguing around the corner.
  • Difficulty: As expected from FromSoftware, **Elden Ring** does not hold the player's hand. The difficulty is high, but it’s fair and rewarding. The challenge isn’t just in combat but also in exploration, as players must solve environmental puzzles and face powerful bosses. The game encourages persistence, and each victory feels earned.
  • RPG Elements: Elden Ring includes the traditional RPG mechanics of leveling up, upgrading weapons, and customizing your character's build. The game’s progression is highly flexible, allowing players to tailor their playstyle to their preferences. Whether you want to be a brute-force warrior, a stealthy assassin, or a master of sorcery, **Elden Ring** provides plenty of options for different playstyles.

Graphics & Sound: 9/10

Visually, **Elden Ring** is stunning. The world is filled with detailed environments that feel both grand and intimate. The **Lands Between** is filled with breathtaking vistas, towering castles, sprawling forests, and eerie catacombs. The attention to detail is extraordinary, and the game’s artistic direction successfully captures the mystical and haunting tone of its world.

  • Art Direction: The art direction is masterful, with **Gothic architecture**, vast landscapes, and haunting creature designs that make every area distinct. The game's visual style can be dark and grim, with an overwhelming sense of decay and ruin, but it’s also full of beauty in its landscapes, making for a visually diverse experience.
  • Sound Design: The soundtrack, composed by **Yuka Kitamura**, is powerful and atmospheric. The music ranges from sweeping orchestral pieces during exploration to intense, epic scores during boss battles. The sound design complements the game’s visuals and enhances the overall atmosphere, with ambient sounds, subtle environmental cues, and distinct creature noises immersing players in the world of the Lands Between.

Bugs & Performance: 8/10

While **Elden Ring** is technically solid, it isn’t without its issues. On high-end PCs and next-gen consoles, the game runs smoothly for the most part, with high frame rates and minimal loading times. However, some players on older hardware have reported occasional stuttering, frame drops, and texture pop-in.

  • Performance: On consoles like the PS5 and Xbox Series X, the game runs well, but some occasional frame drops can occur during intense moments or when large areas are loaded. The PC version performs well, but users with lower-end systems may struggle with stability. Overall, the performance is solid, but not without the occasional hiccup.
  • Bugs: There are some minor glitches and bugs, such as NPCs getting stuck or textures not loading properly, but these are few and far between. The game’s open-world nature means there are bound to be occasional issues, but these are generally not game-breaking and do not detract significantly from the experience.

Replayability & Content: 10/10

One of **Elden Ring’s** strongest points is its replayability. The game features a massive world filled with hidden secrets, side quests, and numerous bosses, ensuring that no two playthroughs will be identical. There are a variety of endings based on player choices and actions, providing motivation to revisit the game multiple times to experience different outcomes.

The world is rich with lore, and every corner of the **Lands Between** feels like it holds a story waiting to be uncovered. The game’s difficulty also makes it highly replayable, as players may return to overcome more challenging bosses, test out different character builds, or explore alternative routes.

Final Thoughts: 9.5/10

**Elden Ring** is a triumph of game design, offering a beautiful and challenging experience that rewards both exploration and combat. FromSoftware’s ability to blend a vast open world with their signature tight combat mechanics results in a game that feels fresh yet familiar to fans of their previous titles. The open-world design and indirect storytelling may not appeal to everyone, but for those willing to dive in, **Elden Ring** is an unforgettable journey.

Pros:

  • Immersive and vast open world filled with detail and secrets.
  • Challenging but fair combat system with numerous playstyle options.
  • Masterful art direction and stunning visuals.
  • Replayable with multiple endings and tons of content.

Cons:

  • Occasional performance issues and minor bugs.
  • The indirect narrative may feel inaccessible for some players.

Verdict:

**Elden Ring** is a masterpiece of exploration and combat, offering a vast, beautifully crafted world with rewarding gameplay. While it may have a few technical hiccups, it remains one of the most engaging and memorable RPGs in recent years, and it’s a must-play for fans of FromSoftware’s games or anyone looking for a deep, challenging experience.
